Validation

How many time we asked our stakeholders to test our progress?

Desk research

The project started with the aim of improving the experience of accessing information for young adults.

Through desk research we explored the current context of news consumption through digital solutions, identifying the following dynamics:

  • fake news
  • polarization
  • clickbait news
  • news bubbles
  • news overpopulation
  • lack of media literacy in the audience
  • fragmented information fruition

  • multitasking fruition

Those aspects were the basis to define interesting opportunities of work and where thus deeply explored with the users.

Field research

We conducted 14 semi-structured interviews with people aged between 18 and 26 years old. The objectives were multiple: to verify the perception of the problem already identified and eventually discover new ones, as well as their view of an optimal information activity and their actual habits.

This allowed us to gain a deeper understanding of the current experience, the pain points most perceived and which is the context of use. We also identified some main clusters of users within the sample interviewed.

Hence the problem statement: we decided to target young adults who believe it is important to be informed, but currently feel a lack of motivation. As the actual experience requires a lot of time and effort to be completed satisfactorily with the depth desired.

Finally, this activity brought us also insights into the range of opportunities and the context on which focus, giving rise, through the design sprint process, to the big idea and the guidelines for its development.

Concept validation

The concept was validated through 11 semi-structured interviews. The objective was to identify whether the solution actually brings added value and how to convey it. As well as gathering insights on some specific aspects.

The idea was presented through a storyboard. We then compared, by asking for a numerical evaluation, the perception of the following problems in the current experience and then in the proposed one:

  • lack of reliability
  • presence of opinions and not facts
  • high cognitive effort required

  • lack of motivation

Finally, they were asked to describe the proposed service through a metaphor.

There was a general improvement in the proposed solution with regard to the above-mentioned problems, with the exception of some concerns about the reliability of the system, due to unclear operation and lack of confidence in the algorithm.

From this activity, we realised how important transparency of the system is for gaining the trust of users. Suggestions were also collected on functions considered to be of interest.

Development iteration

During the implementation of the prototype, a validation activity was carried out with 5 users with the aim of verifying that the functioning of the system was understood and the added value perceived. As well as obtaining feedbacks on the general usage of the app, the organisation of informations and functions proposed.

The user was asked to navigate the app freely, as if he had just downloaded it, without proposing any task, to observe what would be a spontaneous use and perception. Afterwards some questions were asked to check what had been understood and general comments were collected.

Different versions of some of the screens were also proposed in order to check how the system’s perceptions changed by changing the graphic choices and the arrangement of the elements; users were asked to give a score to some adjectives proposed, “reliability” and “captivating”, for the different versions on a numerical scale.

In general, the functioning was understood, although there were a few improvements necessary in the explanation of the backend process. The functions present were appreciated: in particular the dictionary and the news history.

The graphical style preferred by the users between the proposed variants guided the final choices and was defined by them as equally appealing and reliable. Finally, we gathered some insights into what information about the activity of other people could be of interest, with the aim of guiding the choices in completing the activity section.

Final validation

During the project madness the final validation of the project was carried out. The objective was to verify through the final prototype the understanding of the system and the perception of the proposed benefits. The activity was carried out in three phases:

  • A first one designed to remind users of the problems of the current experience.
  • A second phase to use the prototype created on Figma in order to collect feedbacks.

  • A third to explain and demonstrate the functioning of the backend implemented on Python.

Outcomes

The project Madness allowed us to gain relevant insights for our projects. About the prototype testing, the users evidenced some usability issues, as false affordances or information overloading, that were solved in the final prototype version.

About the perceived benefits, it was confirmed that Newsworthy is able to transmit its strong and relevant benefits, which are exalted in the comparison with the current daily information experience of the users.
